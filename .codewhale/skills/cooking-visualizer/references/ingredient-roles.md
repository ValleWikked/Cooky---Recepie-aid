# Ingredient Roles Reference

Use this file when reasoning about Panel 1 content: filling the 6-column quantity
matrix AND the "Variants & Substitutions" section for each ingredient.

The 6 columns are: **0 | ½× | ¾× | 1× | ⁵⁄₄× | 3/2×**

For each ingredient category below, the table gives the verdict and consequence
for each column. The "Zone" field describes where the tolerance zone lies:
WIDE = several adjacent columns give effectively the same result;
NARROW = even one step from 1× is perceptible.

Mark flour and any empirically-determined quantities with `?` at 1×.
Mark estimated air fryer times with `~`.

## Aromatics — Onion

Role: sweetness, aromatic depth, moisture contribution to raw mixtures.

Zone: WIDE below 1× (¾× often indistinguishable from 1×). MODERATE above
(⁵⁄₄× starts introducing excess moisture in patty/ball mixtures).

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Flat taste | No sweet backbone. The dish tastes one-dimensional — the base ingredient dominates without aromatic support. |
| ½×     | Weak background | Subtle hint of sweetness. Texture better in raw mixtures (less moisture). Noticeably mild to most palates. |
| ¾×     | Balanced ✓ | Effectively the same result as 1× for most people. Slightly less sweet, but within the tolerance zone. |
| 1×     | Balanced ✓ | Correct sweetness and aromatic depth. Moisture contribution is accounted for in the recipe. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Starting to water | In raw mixtures (falafel, veggie burgers): onion releases extra liquid → mixture becomes wetter → more binder needed → denser result. In cooked dishes: less critical. |
| 3/2×   | Watery | Clear excess moisture in raw mixtures. Significantly more binder required to compensate. Onion flavour starts to dominate. |

**Form variants:**
- Fresh: standard. Releases moisture when cut or processed — factor this into binding.
- Dried/powder: use ~1 tsp instead of 80 g. No moisture contribution — mixture will be drier, may need less or no binder. Flavour more concentrated and uniform.
- Shallot: more delicate, slightly sweeter, less moisture. Same quantity or 10% more.
- Scallion: milder, less sweet. Use white and light-green parts only, ~60 g instead of 80 g.
- Red onion: sharper raw flavour, more colour. Same quantity.

**Substitutions:**
- Shallot: closest substitute, slightly more refined flavour.
- Onion powder: works if moisture reduction is acceptable.
- Omission: viable in falafel — the dish still works, just less sweet depth.

## Aromatics — Garlic

Role: pungency, aromatic depth, savoury backbone.

Zone: WIDE below 1× (even ½× gives noticeable garlic presence). MODERATE above
(⁵⁄₄× starts dominating other flavours).

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Bland | No garlic pungency. Dish is flat. |
| ½×     | Weak garlic | Barely noticeable. Suitable for garlic-sensitive eaters. |
| ¾×     | Mild background ✓ | Pleasant garlic background without dominating. |
| 1×     | Pronounced ✓ | Bright garlic flavour — classic falafel. |
| 3/2×   | Too much | Garlic bomb. Drowns out all other flavours. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Garlic punch | Garlic dominates. May overpower cumin and coriander. |

**Form variants:**
- Fresh: standard. Intensity varies by variety and age.
- Garlic powder: ~½ tsp instead of 3 cloves. More even distribution.
- Roasted garlic: milder, sweeter. Increase quantity by 1.5×.

**Substitutions:**
- Garlic powder: even distribution, no moisture. ½ tsp per 3 cloves.
- Omission: noticeable but not critical — dish becomes tame.

## Herbs — Parsley (fresh)

Role: freshness, herbal aroma, green colour.

Zone: WIDE — 0 acceptable, 3/2× still fine.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Acceptable ✓ | No herbs. Cleaner taste, paler colour. |
| ½×     | Light hint | Barely noticeable herbal note. |
| ¾×     | Green ✓ | Pleasant herbal background. |
| 1×     | Freshness ✓ | Bright herbs — classic balance. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Herb-heavy | Herbal taste pronounced. Greener colour. |
| 3/2×   | Still fine ✓ | Very green falafel. Acceptable. |

**Form variants:**
- Fresh: standard. Leaves only, no stems.
- Cilantro: brighter, citrusy note. Not universally liked.
- Dried: weaker aroma. Use 1 tbsp instead of a bunch.

**Substitutions:**
- Cilantro: brighter, citrusy note. Same proportion.
- Dill: different profile — anise note. 15 g, not for everyone.
- Omission: fully acceptable. Cleaner taste, less complex.

## Spices — Cumin (ground)

Role: warmth, earthiness, key Middle Eastern note.

Zone: MODERATE below; NARROW above — even slight excess is noticeable.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Bland | No Middle Eastern character. Falafel loses identity. |
| ½×     | Weak background | Subtle hint. Lost against garlic. |
| ¾×     | Mild ✓ | Warm note without dominating. |
| 1×     | Classic ✓ | Correct Middle Eastern profile. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Bitter | Cumin starts to taste bitter. Overpowers coriander. |
| 3/2×   | Bitter smoke | Pronounced bitterness. Dish ruined. |

**Form variants:**
- Ground: standard. Intensity diminishes with storage time.
- Whole seeds: toast and grind for maximum aroma.

**Substitutions:**
- Cumin seeds: toast and grind — brighter aroma. 1 tsp seeds = 1½ tsp ground.
- Omission: NOT RECOMMENDED. Cumin is the key Middle Eastern note.

## Spices — Coriander (ground)

Role: citrusy note, roundness, balances cumin.

Zone: MODERATE in both directions.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Flat-ish | Missing citrus note. Cumin stands out too much. |
| ½×     | Weak citrus | Barely noticeable roundness. |
| ¾×     | Light ✓ | Pleasant balance with cumin. |
| 1×     | Balance ✓ | Correct cumin/coriander ratio. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Citrus dominates | Coriander moves to the foreground. |
| 3/2×   | Soapy taste | Some people have genetic sensitivity to coriander. |

**Form variants:**
- Ground: standard.
- Whole seeds: toast and grind.

**Substitutions:**
- Coriander seeds: toast and grind — more citrusy. ¾ tsp seeds.
- Omission: noticeable — taste becomes less rounded. Cumin will dominate.

## Seasoning — Salt

Role: flavour enhancer, critical balance.

Zone: NARROW both directions — salt is very sensitive.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Bland | No flavour. Dish inedible. |
| ½×     | Under-salted | Flavour muted. Noticeably lacking. |
| ¾×     | Weak | Tolerable, but lacks brightness. |
| 1×     | Balance ✓ | Correct salt level. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Over-salted | Salt clearly felt. Masks spices. |
| 3/2×   | Heavily over-salted | Inedible. |

**Form variants:**
- Fine: standard. Even distribution.
- Coarse (kosher): less dense — 1¼ tsp instead of 1 tsp.
- Sea salt: similar to coarse.

**Substitutions:**
- Soy sauce: different flavour profile. Adds umami. 1 tbsp ≈ 1 tsp salt, but changes taste.

## Seasoning — Black Pepper

Role: light heat, background note.

Zone: VERY WIDE — all 6 columns in tolerance zone.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | OK ✓ | No pepper — taste slightly simpler. |
| ½×     | OK ✓ | Light hint of heat. |
| ¾×     | OK ✓ | Pleasant background. |
| 1×     | Normal ✓ | Classic level. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | OK ✓ | Noticeable heat. |
| 3/2×   | OK ✓ | Spicy, but not critical. |

**Form variants:**
- Ground: standard.
- Freshly ground: more aromatic.
- White pepper: milder, less aromatic.

**Substitutions:**
- White pepper: milder, for light-coloured dishes. Same proportion.
- Cayenne pepper: hotter — different profile. ¼ tsp, use caution.
- Omission: no consequences. Taste slightly simpler.

## Leavening — Baking Powder

Role: lightness, airy texture.

Zone: MODERATE below; NARROW above — excess gives chemical taste.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | OK ✓ | Without baking powder. Falafel slightly denser. |
| ½×     | Weak | Minimal lift effect. |
| ¾×     | Light ✓ | Barely noticeable airiness. |
| 1×     | Normal ✓ | Correct lightness. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Off-taste | Chemical taste begins to appear. |
| 3/2×   | Soapy taste | Pronounced chemical taste. Dish ruined. |

**Form variants:**
- Standard: normal baking powder.

**Substitutions:**
- Baking soda + acid: ¼ tsp soda + ½ tsp lemon juice. Activates immediately.
- Omission: falafel will be denser, but not critical.

## Binder — Flour

Role: binding agent — only if mixture is too wet.

Zone: N/A below 1× (empirical); NARROW above — each spoonful changes texture.

| Column | Verdict | Consequence |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 0      | Ideal | Correct technique — flour not needed. |
| ½×     | Loose | Mixture slightly crumbly when frying. |
| ¾×     | Holds | Minimal binding. |
| 1×     | Bound ✓? | Empirically determined — test with a ball. |
| ⁵⁄₄×   | Dense | Noticeably denser texture. |
| 3/2×   | Brick | Heavy, dense falafel. Loss of lightness. |

**Form variants:**
- Wheat: standard. Each tablespoon significantly changes texture.
- Chickpea flour: enhances flavour coherence. More hygroscopic.
- Cornstarch: binds tighter with less volume.

**Substitutions:**
- Chickpea flour: same role, same range. Gluten-free. Start with ½ the wheat flour amount.
- Cornstarch: use ½ the quantity — binds tighter with less bulk.
- Omission: the correct answer if technique is right. Always try zero flour first and test.
